We kept on towards one of the outer islands, where Gus’ lab was.

Unlike the rest of Atlantis, it was not built from cloth and pole. It was metal and glass. A modern structure. The islands were like this. They existed as modern facilities. Powered, bright, a takeaway from Main State. I felt at ease, and uncomfortable at the same time, like an imposter, as if they’d recognize me as an outsider amidst my natural habitat.

“Here we are. This is where we do our good work.”

“Morning Gus.”

“Morning Ferguson.”

“Frank. This is great material. The stand up is going to kill.”

I looked down at him as I pulled him from the bag. This would be the second time I let him out of my sights since we started. The first time he was almost eaten.

“It’ll be alright. Sonny will take care of you.”

When you make mistakes on purpose, and you make them carefully, you can push supplies to somewhere very specific, places that don’t have good medical care, places that are underprivileged, or where large populations outnumber capable medical professionals, and even places off the grid, in the middle of nowhere, like this.”

Maybe after the surgery would I finally be able to have some of these unknowns come to light. That they would be destroyed along with Toki, on the other side of those walls as I waited. I watched Gus as he fumbled with is pen.
